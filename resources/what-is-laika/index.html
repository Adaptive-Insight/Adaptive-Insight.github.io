What is Laika?

Laika is a private AI finder for your Mac. It finds relevant documents and photos first, then answers only from the sources you choose.

Adaptive Insight July 12, 2026 3 min read

Traditional file search expects you to remember a filename. A broad AI answer box has the opposite problem: it may read far more than the question requires. Laika separates those jobs.

Why find comes first

Laika turns the folders and photos you choose into a local catalog. It can search names, metadata, extracted text, OCR, and visual labels without scanning every source for every query. Results arrive as files you can inspect, not an opaque workspace-wide answer.

Only after you choose evidence does Laika read more deeply. That keeps routine discovery responsive and makes the boundary of an answer visible before model work begins.

How Laika works

1. Build a durable local catalog

Documents contribute metadata, extracted text, and page anchors. Photos contribute metadata, thumbnails, OCR, and visual labels. Searchable content is stored in SQLite and survives app or Mac restarts.

2. Find and preview

Collection search is index-first. Laika returns ranked documents and photos with previews and match context. A small on-device intent parse can refine a submitted search, but it does not need to read candidate files before showing results.

3. Choose the answer scope

Laika keeps three states distinct: focus controls the preview, selection fills the visible tray, and scope defines what an Ask action may use. Clicking a result never silently changes the answer boundary.

4. Answer from selected evidence

Direct facts use source-backed evidence when available. Short synthesis stays tied to approved quotes, and deeper reading stays inside the selected files. Answers include citations. If the evidence is insufficient, Laika says so instead of silently widening the search.

Private by design

The catalog, previews, evidence, and model execution stay on your Mac after the model is installed. The alpha has no account, telemetry, crash reporting, or automatic update checks.

The first model-backed action downloads and verifies the promoted model. iCloud originals are fetched only when you explicitly open or export them. With Redact on export enabled, Laika creates a protected copy without changing the original.

Alpha boundaries

  • The first model warm can take roughly 10 to 30 seconds.
  • Large collections need time for their first enrichment pass.
  • OCR is currently English-centric.
  • DOCX and XLSX are not yet indexed.
  • Live answer streaming is not enabled in this build.
